# Module: advisor-crosscheck

**Switch on when:** the household works with a financial advisor (or planner/CPA who does planning). This module frames the whole review as a *second opinion* rather than a replacement.

## PROCESS.md additions

### Advisor relationship (framing rule, near the top of the document)
- This analysis **supplements — and is explicitly compared against — advice from the household's professional advisor** ({{ADVISOR — name/firm; verify current at each review}}). Where recommendations diverge from professional advice, the divergence itself is a useful discussion topic, not a verdict.
- The spending target and projection results double as **validation inputs to the advisor's planning model** — they must be defensible line by line.

### (under "Standing assumptions & treatment rules")
- Maintain the advisor's contact details in the report header and verify them annually (matters for the survivor scenario — the surviving spouse needs to know who to call).

## Report sections (two)

**Model cross-check: advisor vs. this review** — divergence table (assumption or recommendation, advisor's position, this review's position, why they differ, who should win), filled/updated after the advisor session.

**Open questions for the advisor** — the review's outputs reframed as an agenda for the next advisor meeting. *(This is often the single most valuable page in the report.)*
