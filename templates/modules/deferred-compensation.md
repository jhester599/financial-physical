# Module: deferred-compensation

**Switch on when:** any earner participates in a nonqualified deferred compensation (NQDC) plan — sometimes called an "executive deferral plan," "excess plan," or "top-hat plan."

## PROCESS.md additions (under "Standing assumptions & treatment rules")

### Deferred compensation (NQDC)
- NQDC balances are **unsecured employer liabilities** — the employee is a general creditor. Include the balance in the employer-concentration measure (if that module is on), not just the investment tally, and note employer credit quality annually.
- **Distribution elections (timing and form) are a primary tax lever.** Analyze the current election against projected retirement-year brackets each year. Election changes are rigidly constrained by Section 409A (the "12-month/5-year rule") — never assume an election can be changed casually; check the plan's rules and deadlines.
- **Current election:** {{NQDC_ELECTION — form and timing, e.g., "10-year installments at separation"; or "to confirm from plan statement"}}.
- Model the payout stream explicitly in the projection — it changes which accounts get tapped when in early retirement. If installments span 10+ years, note the potential state-tax sourcing benefit if a move to a lower-tax state is plausible (federal law shields ≥10-year installment streams from the former work-state).
- **Deferral-rate decision:** analyze the annual election window each year — deferral saves current taxes but grows the unsecured exposure; the right rate depends on bracket spread and concentration policy.
